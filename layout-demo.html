Hero Image

When touch becomes language — this project explores gesture as a design medium. How can interaction move beyond tap and swipe into something more expressive, physical, and alive?

"The best gesture disappears. You stop thinking about the hand and start thinking about the intention."

Three gesture systems were explored: pressure-sensitive surfaces, directional swipe libraries, and continuous motion tracking — each tested against real user scenarios to understand where gesture adds clarity and where it creates friction.

Main image

Research insight

Users already have embodied expectations — lifting, pushing, pulling. Design should align with those instincts, not fight them.

Image
Design

Three gesture systems

Each system was prototyped at high fidelity with spring physics and custom easing curves to simulate physical weight and resistance — the difference between a drawer that floats and one that feels anchored.
